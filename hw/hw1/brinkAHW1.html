The following considers the proportions relative to the column sums, which are left and right momhandedness respectively. This is helpful in determining if selfhandedness and momhandedness are independent since this gives the probability of selfhandedness given the specified momhandedness. The table with no margins can be used to determin the probability of the specified selfhandedness. If these probabilities are not equal then we know that selfhandedness and momhandedness are not independent.

prop.table(xtabs(~selfhandedness + momhandedness, data=twiceCleanedSS), margin=2)
##               momhandedness
## selfhandedness         L         R
##              L 0.0625000 0.1145038
##              R 0.9375000 0.8854962

Exercise 9 I do not think that momhandedness is particularly useful in explaining selfhandedness since both being left-handed is lower in the proportions than being opposite. Nevertheless, the probability that a child is left-handed given that the mom is right-handed is 0.1079 which does not equal the probability of 0.942446 that a mom is right-handed. This indicates that there is not necessarily independence. On the other hand, the proportion of left-handed children from left-handed mothers is 0.0625000 while the proportion of left-handed children from right-handed mothers is 0.1145038. Since these proportions are not equal, this indicates that the mother’s handedness does not explain the handedness of the children.
